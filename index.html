About Me

I am a first year Ph.D. student in the School of Computer Science at the College of Computing at Georgia Tech. As a graduate research assistant, I currently study information security, and advised by Wenke Lee in the Institute for Information Security & Privacy (IISP).

Before that, I received my M.S. degree in Computer Science in Georgia Tech under the supervision of Wenke Lee in 2018. Prior joining GaTech, I had pursued professional career (as electrical engineer on the second biggest telecomunication company in europe) for 3 years. I recieved my B.S. degree from the Electrical and Electronics Engineering Department at TOBB ETU in 2012.

Research Interest

My primary research area is security and machine learning. Specifically, I am working on adversarial machine learning and resilient machine learning models for malware family classification. I am studying types of algorithms used in machine learning and adapt the appropriate algorithms for graph based analysis.
